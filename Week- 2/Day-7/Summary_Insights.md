# AFL EDA Summary

## 5 Key Insights
1. **Legacy Teams Hold the Most Data:** Teams like Collingwood, Carlton, and Essendon dominate both the total games played and number of player records, making them ideal targets for historical marketing campaigns.
2. **Career Longevity is Capped:** The distribution of `last_age` shows a steep decline after age 32. Clubs should prioritize list management strategies that heavily weight draft picks and younger talent as veterans approach this threshold.
3. **Physical Standardization Across Teams:** Player weight distributions are remarkably consistent across the top 15 teams (medians around 80-85kg). Physical conditioning benchmarks are highly standardized across the league.
4. **Competitive Fantasy Output:** Average fantasy points across top teams are very tight, indicating that statistical dominance is evenly spread. Fantasy pricing models should rely more on player-specific matchups than broad team averages.
5. **Modern Data is More Robust:** There's a stark increase in seasonal records over time, plateauing in recent decades. Any predictive modeling for current games should heavily discount early historical data due to its sparsity.
